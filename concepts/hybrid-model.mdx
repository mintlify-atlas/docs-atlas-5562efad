---
title: 'Hybrid model'
description: 'How BR-25 combines Poisson goal prediction with Elo strength adjustment to produce match outcome probabilities.'
---

The BR-25 prediction engine uses a two-stage hybrid architecture. The first stage uses a **Poisson model** to estimate how many goals each team is likely to score based on historical goal rates. The second stage applies an **Elo adjustment** that shifts those estimates up or down depending on the relative strength difference between the two teams.

Neither model alone is sufficient:

- Poisson captures scoring patterns and home/away context, but ignores overall team quality trends.
- Elo captures cumulative form and head-to-head strength, but says nothing about how goals are distributed.

Combining them produces more calibrated outcome probabilities than either model produces on its own.

## Stage 1 ŌĆö Poisson base goal estimates

For each match, the model computes a base expected goals value for each team using attack and defense strength indices derived from historical data:

```text
gols_base_casa      = ataque_casa      ├Ś defesa_visitante ├Ś media_gols_liga_casa
gols_base_visitante = ataque_visitante ├Ś defesa_casa      ├Ś media_gols_liga_fora
```

Where:

- `ataque_casa` ŌĆö home team's average goals scored at home, normalized by league average
- `defesa_visitante` ŌĆö away team's average goals conceded away, normalized by league average
- `media_gols_liga_casa` ŌĆö league-wide average goals scored by home teams

See [Poisson distribution](/concepts/poisson-distribution) for how the strength indices are computed.

## Stage 2 ŌĆö Elo adjustment

The Elo rating difference between the two teams is used to produce a multiplicative adjustment factor that is applied to the Poisson base estimates:

```text
elo_diff             = (rating_casa + vantagem_casa) - rating_visitante
fator_ajuste_casa    = 1 + (elo_diff / 1000) ├Ś ELO_INFLUENCE
fator_ajuste_visitante = 1 - (elo_diff / 1000) ├Ś ELO_INFLUENCE
```

With `ELO_INFLUENCE = 0.10`, a 100-point Elo gap shifts the expected goal totals by 1%. A 500-point gap shifts them by 5%. The adjustment is intentionally small ŌĆö Poisson carries most of the signal.

```python
ELO_INFLUENCE = 0.10

elo_diff = (rating_casa + vantagem_time_casa) - rating_visitante
fator_ajuste_casa = 1 + (elo_diff / 1000) * ELO_INFLUENCE
fator_ajuste_visitante = 1 - (elo_diff / 1000) * ELO_INFLUENCE

gols_finais_casa = gols_base_casa * max(0.1, fator_ajuste_casa)
gols_finais_visitante = gols_base_visitante * max(0.1, fator_ajuste_visitante)
```

<Note>
The `max(0.1, ...)` floor prevents the adjusted expected goals from collapsing to zero or going negative when the Elo gap is very large.
</Note>

## Probability accumulation

Once the final expected goals `╬╗_casa` and `╬╗_visitante` are computed, the model enumerates all scorelines up to `POISSON_MAX_GOLS = 8` goals per team and accumulates outcome probabilities:

```python
for g_c in range(POISSON_MAX_GOLS + 1):       # 0..8 goals for home team
    for g_v in range(POISSON_MAX_GOLS + 1):   # 0..8 goals for away team
        prob_placar = poisson(gols_finais_casa, g_c) * poisson(gols_finais_visitante, g_v)
        if g_c > g_v:
            prob_vitoria_casa += prob_placar
        elif g_c == g_v:
            prob_empate += prob_placar
        else:
            prob_vitoria_visitante += prob_placar
```

This produces three probabilities: home win, draw, and away win. Because the grid is truncated at 8 goals, scorelines with 9 or more goals are excluded. The combined probability mass of those scorelines is negligible in practice.

<Tip>
The grid approach also gives you scoreline-level probabilities as a byproduct ŌĆö `prob_placar` for each `(g_c, g_v)` pair is the probability of that exact scoreline.
</Tip>

## Constants

| Constant | Value | Role |
|---|---|---|
| `ELO_RATING_INICIAL` | 1500 | Starting Elo for new teams |
| `ELO_K_FACTOR_BASE` | 30 | Base Elo update magnitude |
| `ELO_VANTAGEM_CASA_PADRAO` | 30 | Fallback home advantage when no history exists |
| `POISSON_MAX_GOLS` | 8 | Grid truncation limit per team |
| `ELO_INFLUENCE` | 0.10 | Weight of Elo adjustment on expected goals |
