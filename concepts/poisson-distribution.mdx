The Poisson distribution models the probability of a given number of discrete events occurring within a fixed interval, assuming events happen independently at a constant average rate. In football, goals scored in a match closely follow this distribution, making it a natural choice for predicting scorelines.

## The Poisson formula

The probability of exactly `k` goals given an expected rate `λ` is:

```text
P(k; λ) = e^(-λ) × λ^k / k!
```

In code:

```python
import math

def poisson(lmbda, k):
    """Calculates probability of k occurrences for mean lmbda."""
    return math.exp(-lmbda) * (lmbda ** k) / math.factorial(k)
```

For example, if a team is expected to score `λ = 1.5` goals:

- `P(0) ≈ 0.223` — probability of scoring 0 goals
- `P(1) ≈ 0.335` — probability of scoring 1 goal
- `P(2) ≈ 0.251` — probability of scoring 2 goals
- `P(3) ≈ 0.126` — probability of scoring 3 goals

## Computing attack and defense strength indices

The expected goals value `λ` for each team in a given match is not constant — it is derived from that team's historical attacking and defensive performance, normalized against the league average.

The function `calcular_forcas_poisson` computes four indices per team:

| Index | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `ataque_casa` | Ratio of goals scored at home to league home average |
| `defesa_casa` | Ratio of goals conceded at home to league away average |
| `ataque_fora` | Ratio of goals scored away to league away average |
| `defesa_fora` | Ratio of goals conceded away to league home average |


An index value of `1.0` means average performance. A value of `1.3` means 30% above average; `0.7` means 30% below average.

## Expected goals per match

Once the strength indices are available, expected goals for a specific match are computed as:

```text
gols_base_casa      = ataque_casa(home) × defesa_fora(away) × media_gols_casa
gols_base_visitante = ataque_fora(away) × defesa_casa(home) × media_gols_fora
```

This combines the home team's attacking threat, the away team's defensive weakness, and the overall league scoring context into a single expected goals number.

## Grid truncation at 8 goals

The model enumerates scorelines on a grid from 0 to `POISSON_MAX_GOLS = 8` goals per team. This means scorelines with 9 or more goals are excluded.

<Info>
Truncating at 8 goals captures over 99.9% of the probability mass for typical `λ` values in the Campeonato Brasileiro, where expected goals rarely exceed 3.0 per team.
</Info>

The Poisson distribution assigns non-zero probability to arbitrarily large `k`, but probabilities fall off rapidly. Setting `POISSON_MAX_GOLS = 8` is a practical engineering decision that avoids an infinite loop without meaningfully distorting the outcome probabilities.

## Assumptions

<Note>
The Poisson model assumes that goals scored by the home team and goals scored by the away team are **statistically independent**. In practice, there is a small correlation — teams that concede early tend to open up and concede more — but the independence assumption is a standard simplification that performs well empirically.
</Note>

Other assumptions:

- Historical goal averages are a reasonable predictor of future performance.
- League averages are computed across all matches in the input dataset and treated as fixed.
- Teams with no historical data (empty fixture list) default to an index of `1.0`.
