Expected goals (xG) is the number of goals the Poisson model predicts a club would score based on their attack strength, their opponent's defense strength, and league-wide scoring averages. It is a model output, not a count of actual goals scored.

## xG vs actual goals

| Metric | What it measures |
|--------|------------------|
| Actual goals | Goals scored in real matches |
| xG (expected goals) | Goals predicted by the hybrid model given the same fixture list |

A club with high xG but modest actual goals may be underperforming relative to their underlying offensive strength. A club with low xG but many actual goals may be overperforming. Over a full season the two numbers tend to converge.

<Info>
xG in Sassamaru BR-25 is derived from Poisson attack and defense force parameters, not from shot-level event data. It reflects structural offensive strength rather than shot quality.
</Info>

## The two xG metrics

The ranking display reports two figures for each club:

- **xG Total** — the sum of expected goals across all matches processed. A higher value indicates a club faced many matches or generated high offensive output throughout the dataset.
- **xG Médio/Jogo** — xG Total divided by the number of matches played. This is the per-game average and is the better indicator of consistent offensive strength, independent of how many games the club played.

<Tip>
When comparing clubs that played different numbers of matches, use xG Médio/Jogo. Use xG Total when you want to understand overall season-level output.
</Tip>

## Using `calcular_xg_por_clube` programmatically

You can call the xG ranking function directly in Python without the GUI:

```python
import pandas as pd
from sassamaru import calcular_xg_por_clube

df = pd.read_csv('sassamaru-br-25/campeonato-brasileiro-full.csv')
xg_total, xg_medio = calcular_xg_por_clube(df)

# Print ranking sorted by total xG
ranking = sorted(xg_total.items(), key=lambda x: x[1], reverse=True)
for clube, xg in ranking:
    print(f"{clube:20} | {xg:8.2f} | {xg_medio[clube]:6.2f}")
```

## How xG is computed internally

For each row in the match DataFrame, the function calls `prever_partida_hibrido` to obtain the hybrid model prediction. The predicted goals for both the home and away club are accumulated:

```python
for _, row in df.iterrows():
    res = prever_partida_hibrido(mandante, visitante, {}, forcas_poisson, medias_liga, {})
    if res:
        xg_por_clube[mandante] += res['gols_esperados_mandante']
        xg_por_clube[visitante] += res['gols_esperados_visitante']
        jogos_por_clube[mandante] += 1
        jogos_por_clube[visitante] += 1
```

The Poisson force parameters (`forcas_poisson`) are estimated from the same dataset via `calcular_forcas_poisson(df)`, so xG rankings reflect the historical attack-defense equilibrium in the data.
